New Zealand

A charming oasis in the Pacific, New Zealand is renowned for its breathtaking scenery, which includes pristine beaches, lush forests, and towering mountains. With everything from exhilarating outdoor pursuits like hiking and bungee jumping to a rich Māori culture and hospitable locals, it is the ideal combination of action and tranquility. New Zealand enthralls the heart and uplifts the spirit with its dynamic cities, iconic movie sets, and dedication to sustainability.

The top tourist destinations in New Zealand

Abel Tasman National Park

Abel Tasman is all about the golden beaches, turquoise seas, and forested hiking trails. For longer hikes, enter the park from the tiny town of Marahau. You can also enter the park by kayaking from Kaiteriteri.

Aoraki Mackenzie International Dark Sky Reserve

The Aoraki Mackenzie International Dark Sky Reserve is one of the largest dark sky reserves in the world. The central South Island, far from any major settlements, is almost entirely free of light pollution, making it an incredible destination for stargazing.

Coromandel Peninsula

The Coromandel Peninsula reaches 50 miles into the Hauraki Gulf, across the Firth of Thames from Auckland. It’s a microcosm of all that’s good in northern New Zealand—stunning beaches, hiking trails, and arty, laid-back towns.

Kaikoura

On the eastern coast of the upper South Island, Kaikoura is famous for its whale and dolphin watching. It’s a marine-life hotspot because of the unique currents and deep trench just offshore. Whale-watching cruises operate all year, and while sperm whale sightings are never guaranteed, there’s a very high chance you’ll see them and dolphins, seals, and albatross.

Rotorua

On the central North Island, Rotorua is famous for its geothermal features and Maori culture. Travelers short on time can visit on a day trip from Auckland, but it’s a convenient stop when traveling through the North Island. Hell’s Gate, Wai-O-Tapu, or Orakei Korako are good options for bubbling mud pools, boiling geysers, and colorful rock formations, and many resorts and holiday parks in the area have hot spring bathing facilities.

Queenstown

Queenstown is not a typical Kiwi town—its real estate is among the most expensive in the country, and the style is more designer than farmer. But there’s no denying that Queenstown is blessed with beautiful geography, as it's set on Lake Wakatipu with views of the Remarkables mountain range. Take the cable car to the top of the hill behind the city for spectacular views.

Let's Go to New Zealand!

The people of New Zealand are the essence of the country. A warm welcome awaits you from amiable Kiwis who are eager to tell you their stories and show you the splendor of their country. Experience the rhythm of the haka, learn about customs that have been passed down through the years, and immerse yourself in Māori culture.
